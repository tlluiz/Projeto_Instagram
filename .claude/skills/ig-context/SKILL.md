# ig-context — the lens for reading Reels

This skill is **not** an analysis. It is the shared lens. Every `ig-analyze-*`
skill loads this first so it judges a creator's transcription by **Instagram's
rules**, not by the rules of a formal essay. On Instagram, fragmented,
repetitive, fast, casual and "incorrect" speech is often *exactly right*.

Apply everything below as the régua (yardstick) when analyzing.

## The platform reality

- The feed is an infinite scroll of competing videos. The only currency is
  **attention held over time** — watch time, completion rate and especially
  **rewatches**. The algorithm pushes what people finish, rewatch, **comment**,
  **save** and **share** (sends matter more than likes).
- Sound is usually on; the first frame and the first spoken words land together.
- The viewer did not choose this video — they're judging in real time whether to
  keep scrolling. Every second must *earn* the next one.

## The grammar of virality

1. **Gancho (hook) — the first 1–3 seconds.** If the opening line doesn't stop
   the scroll, nothing else matters. The hook creates a reason to stay: a
   question, a bold/controversial claim, a warning, a curiosity gap, a callout
   ("se você faz isso..."), a promise/result, a number ("3 coisas que...").
2. **Retenção.** After the hook, attention is held with **pattern interrupts**
   (cuts, tone/pace changes), **lacunas de curiosidade** (open loops you must
   stay for), **cliffhangers**, **re-ganchos** mid-video, and constant forward
   pull. Dead air or a slow middle = scroll.
3. **Loops & payoffs.** Strong Reels open a loop early and pay it off late —
   often looping the ending back to the beginning so the video replays
   seamlessly (rewatches boost reach).
4. **CTA / fechamento.** The close is usually a soft engagement nudge — a
   question to drive **comentários**, "salva esse vídeo", "marca alguém",
   "segue pra parte 2" — not a hard sell.

## Tone and voice

- **Casual, lo-fi, authentic > polished.** It should feel like a person talking
  to *one* friend, not a brand broadcasting. Imperfection reads as real.
- Direct address ("você", "mano", "gente", "ó"), short sentences, spoken rhythm,
  filler and self-interruption are features, not bugs.
- **Relatability and deboche/humor** carry a lot of BR short-form. So do
  contrarian takes and "ninguém te conta isso" framings.

## Cultura de internet BR

- Gírias, bordões, memes, regionalismos and current references signal that the
  creator is *inside* the culture. Judge vocabulary and references against this,
  not against formal Portuguese.
- Comment triggers often exploit shared in-group knowledge (a meme, a polêmica,
  a "qual é o seu?").

## The yardstick (use this when scoring anything)

- Reward what *works on Instagram*: a fast hook, relentless retention, a clean
  loop, a comment-driving close, a voice that feels human and BR-native.
- Do **not** penalize informality, repetition, fragments, slang or palavrões —
  on this platform they usually help.
- Always tie a judgment back to a mechanic above ("this works because it opens a
  curiosity gap in the first 2s"), never to generic 'good writing'.
